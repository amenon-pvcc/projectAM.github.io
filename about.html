For Government Censorship and National Security:

  • Council on Foreign Relations (CFR) – They provide information on cyber warfare and government surveillance measures in the name of national security.
    • CFR - Cybersecurity Threats
  • United Nations Counter-Terrorism Office – Discusses the global efforts by governments to curb online terrorism-related content and how internet restrictions are implemented to counter violent extremism.
    • UN Counter-Terrorism: Internet and Security
  • European Union Agency for Cybersecurity (ENISA) – Offers reports on how governments in the EU use internet censorship to protect critical infrastructure and mitigate cyberattacks.ENISA - Protecting Critical Infrastructure
  • Global Network Initiative (GNI) – Focuses on issues related to freedom of expression and privacy in light of government internet regulations.GNI - National Security and Internet Censorship

    For Political and Social Control Through Censorship:

  • Freedom House – Provides detailed reports on internet freedom worldwide, focusing on how governments use censorship to control political discourse, elections, and social narratives.
    • Freedom House - Freedom on the Net
  • Human Rights Watch (HRW) – Covers the impact of internet censorship on human rights, freedom of speech, and political dissent, particularly in authoritarian regimes.
    • HRW - Internet Censorship
  • The Brookings Institution – Analyzes how countries like Turkey, China, and Russia use internet restrictions to suppress political opposition and maintain social order.
    • Brookings - Internet Censorship in Authoritarian Regimes
  • The Berkman Klein Center at Harvard University – Researches online censorship related to political movements and civil unrest globally, highlighting how different countries handle digital freedoms.
    • Berkman Klein Center - Internet Governance
  • OpenNet Initiative – A project that tracks internet censorship and surveillance worldwide, with particular focus on how different governments implement social and political control through online restrictions.
    • OpenNet Initiative
